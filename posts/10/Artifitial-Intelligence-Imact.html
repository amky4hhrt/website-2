Artifitial Intelligence Imact on Daily Workload


The advent of artifitial intelligence (AI) has brought a significant change in the landscape of work and employment. 

The potential of AI to transform the workplace is undeniable, with its ability to automate the routine tasks and enhance productivity.

A report by Goldmon Sachs suggests that AI could replace the equivalent of 300 million full-time jobs by 2030, affecting a quarter of work tasks in the United States and Europe.

The debate on AI's impact on jobs is not black and white. While some view AI as a threat to job security, others see it as an opportunity to alleviate mundate tasks and focus on more fulfilling tasks.

Inclusivity is another area where AI is progressing. There are tools which are developed to assist workers with disabilities are becoming mainstream.

The future of work with AI is a choice between two distinct paths, one that leads to automation and potential job displacement, and another that fosters human-AI collaboration, creating a more dynamic and inclusive work environment.

The impact of AI on workload is not predetermined; it is shaped by our choices and actions today. The potential for AI to revolutionize the workplace is immense, and with thoughtful implementation, it can lead to a more efficient, inclusive and fulfilling work experience for everyone.
